What Are Episodic and Continuous Tasks?

Episodic tasks are discrete activities with a clear beginning and end, such as solving a puzzle or playing a game of chess. These tasks are relevant in Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) because they can be modeled as finite episodes, allowing for policy optimization and decision-making within a well-defined scope.

Continuous tasks, on the other hand, are ongoing activities without a clear end, such as optimizing traffic signal timings or monitoring stock market data. These tasks are modeled in MDPs as infinite horizon problems, requiring policies that balance long-term and short-term objectives effectively.
